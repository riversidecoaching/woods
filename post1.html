Post 1


The interpretation board gives a brief history of the site, which was part of a much larger wood for many hundreds of years. It was cleared in the 18th century but the ancient hedges are remnants of the original woodland. The main planting of native local trees was in winter 1998 and spring 1999, with later plantings in odd corners. Large species such as ash and oak are generally in the centre of planted areas with shrubs such as hazel, spindle, dog rose, wild privet, dogwood, guelder rose and wayfaring tree on the outside.
