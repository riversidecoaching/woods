In winter the buds are green; the only other green winter buds you will find at Filnore are sycamore. Higher up the slope are two mature oak trees. One is alive and well and a great source of food for many species of insects, spiders, birds and mammals. The other oak lies dead on the ground. We have left it there as a home for fungi, beetles and all the creatures that depend on dead wood as a food source. Many of our most beautiful beetles spend years inside dead wood as fat white grubs. There is bracken growing here which can become invasive, stifling other plants. It is one of six different ferns growing at Filnore.
